# Censoring evidence — 4096-cap nulls are not attractors

## Claim

A response that reaches the 4,096-token generation ceiling with `finish_reason=length` and null visible content is **right-censored**. It proves only that the run had not emitted a visible answer by the cap. It does not prove that 4,096 tokens are the model's natural stopping point, that the hidden trace is stable there, or that training created a 4,096-token attractor.

The campaign therefore excludes capped/null rows from uncensored convergence statistics. They remain visible in failure and correctness accounting, but their observed length is treated as a lower bound.

## Why this is censoring rather than a 4096 attractor

1. **The cap is imposed externally.** `finish_reason=length` is generated by the serving limit, not by a model stop token.
2. **Uncapping moves the endpoint.** The rows that were problematic at 4,096 continue to distinct natural stops at 3,563, 4,032, 5,936, 6,256, and 12,878 completion tokens under the uncapped diagnostic. A true 4,096 attractor would not be established by cap termination alone.
3. **The same row can recover behaviorally.** HumanEval/132 at exploratory step8 naturally stops at 3,563 total tokens, emits content, and passes base tests. That is incompatible with treating its earlier capped/null observation as a measured natural endpoint.
4. **The dose response is not monotone toward 4,096.** HumanEval/116 moves 6,553 → 3,745 → 5,980 reasoning tokens; HumanEval/132 moves 5,753 → 12,688 → 3,350. The evidence is task- and dose-dependent convergence, not collapse to one cap value.
5. **Trace structure changes.** HumanEval/132 step8 uses 16 reasoning paragraphs versus 35 at step0. The step4 outlier is one approximately 15K-character runaway paragraph, not a repeated loop. The trace-forensics payload is `420523724962c63b3efadecd20be74eedfc1c04f34186f810575541594956a10`.

These facts justify the narrow statement that 4,096-cap nulls are censored observations. They do not justify claiming every long trace is good, or that the uncapped endpoint is deterministic.

## Greedy same-serve variance

Greedy decoding does not make hidden-reasoning length deterministic on this runtime. The completed step4+1 duplicate panel observed:

| Task | Replicate 1 | Replicate 2 | Absolute pair delta |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| HumanEval/116 | 4,492 | 3,664 | 18.43% |
| HumanEval/132 | 5,476 | 3,979 | 27.34% |
| HumanEval/99 | 1,072 | 737 | **31.25%** |

The conservative duplicate-variance floor is therefore **31.25%**, the maximum absolute within-serve replicate delta. The summary receipt is `010e4c1eff683a7595d39c4685272766bfc40995b858835e1e56ad73521db9af`; its generation payload is `517c671ebd574e7751cf919c4a043f5aad164b469dd4bc7a1514f4746ba086a0`.

## Preregistered panel rule

A directional transfer signal is promotable only when all of the following are true:

1. the 12-unique-prompt median reasoning delta versus step0 is negative;
2. at least 8 of 12 unique prompts decrease; and
3. the absolute median decrease exceeds the conservative maximum duplicate-pair variance floor.

For the completed step4+1 panel:

- median reasoning delta: **-17.812205477987973%**;
- sign count: **8 decreased / 4 increased**;
- duplicate-variance floor: **31.25%**;
- rule result: **FAIL** because `17.8122 < 31.25`.

Therefore the panel supports a descriptive negative median, but not a transfer promotion. A two-prompt read, a capped/null response, or a negative median smaller than same-serve variation cannot override this rule.

## Reporting rule

Publish capped/null rows as `>=4096, right-censored, no visible content`. Do not substitute `4096` into an uncensored mean or median, infer a natural stopping point, or call the cap an attractor. Any convergence claim must identify whether it uses capped or uncapped rows and must carry panel size, median, sign count, duplicate variance, and receipt identity.
